Background Research

Nicotine (C₁₀H₁₄N₂)

According to cancer.gov, a tobacco cigarette is a product which is mainly created from tobacco. It is shaped in a cylindrical shape and inside it, there are cut and cured tobacco leaves. It is all wrapped in a thin paper, mostly rolling paper. According to lung.org, tobacco cigarettes contain more than 600 substances and when it’s burned, they produce more than 7000 substances. Other than that, more than 69 substances from tobacco cigarettes are carcinogens or substances that can cause cancer.

The three main components from all of the 7000 substances are Nicotine, Tar, and Carbon Monoxide. Nicotine is the substance found in tobacco that causes addiction and pleasure. The pleasing effects are temporary, thus it makes the smoker reach for another cigarette and never stop smoking. Next is Tar. Tar is a substance that creates stains in the smoker’s teeth and fingers. It contains carcinogens or substances that can cause cancer. Besides that, it can damage the bronchioles, the small tubes in our lungs, as well as cilia, the small hair in our lungs. If we keep consuming tar, it can lead to chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) and emphysema. The last substance is Carbon Monoxide. Carbon Monoxide are toxic gases, usually found from vehicles, which replaces the oxygen in blood. It makes the heart and lungs work harder and the cells in our body don't receive the oxygen needed. This will lead to diseases like stroke, as well as, baby miscarriage.

Nicotine (C₁₀H₁₄N₂)

According to medicalnewstoday.com, an electronic cigarette is a device that produces a vaporized solution, mostly nicotine, to be inhaled. It simulates the sensation of smoking tobacco cigarettes but without the smoke.

Electronic cigarettes have 3 main components: battery, atomizer, and the E-Liquid or E-juice. There are 3 common types of battery for electronic cigarettes. First is disposable. Some electronic cigarettes, mostly the old ones, have a limited lifespan. Thus, they need to be thrown away after being used and they are not rechargeable. The second one is integrated. The cigarettes that use this type of battery cannot be replaced or removed, but different from disposable, integrated batteries can be recharged even though it can only be recharged certain times. The last type is removable. This type of battery can be recharged and replaced or removed after they no longer recharge. The second component of electronic cigarettes is the atomizer. An atomizer is the device to heat up the E-Liquid and convert it into a gas. Atomizer consists of a metal coil and a wick. The process on how it works is such: the battery heats up the metal coil, the E-Liquid gets absorbed into the wick and gets heated. After being heated, the E-Liquid will turn into a gas through the evaporation process. The last component is E-Liquid. E-Liquid is the liquid that gets heated and converted into aerosol or gas. The main ingredients of E-Liquid are water, nicotine, flavorings, and propylene glycol or vegetable glycerin base

There are several differences between tobacco and electronic cigarettes. The first difference is how they work. Tobacco cigarettes produce smoke by burning tobacco. On the other hand, electronic cigarettes produce aerosol or gas by heating up the E-Liquid inside the cigarette. The second difference is the toxicness of both cigarettes. According to scottrobertslaw.com, tobacco cigarettes are way more toxic than electronic cigarettes. The tobacco itself is toxic when burnt. It contains hundreds of toxic chemicals and several carcinogenic chemicals. Meanwhile, electronic cigarettes are healthier than tobacco cigarettes, even though they are still harmful. The E-Liquid doesn’t contain any harmful chemicals like tobacco and all of the ingredients aren’t toxic when vaporized. The other difference is the cost of the cigarettes. Tobacco cigarettes are generally cheaper than electronic cigarettes. Besides that, electronic cigarettes need more cleaning and maintenance than disposable tobacco cigarettes.

According to cdc.gov, smoking can lead to several serious diseases in almost all organs in our body. It can cause cancer, heart disease, stroke, lung diseases, diabetes, and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), like emphysema, chronic bronchitis, etc. Besides that, smoking cigarettes can increase the risk of tuberculosis, several eye diseases, problems in the immune system, as well as rheumatoid arthritis. Passive smoking can also affect health. About 41,000 people died due to passive smoking or secondhand smoking. In addition, there are 400 deaths of infants (babies) every year. Besides causing diseases, smoking can cause addiction. The addiction is caused by nicotine. When we consume nicotine, our brain releases dopamine. It’s the chemical that gives pleasure. When the effect of nicotine starts to disappear, the brain will release more dopamine. The more we smoke, then our sensitivity will decrease. To feel good, we need more dopamine than before. This situation will make us hard to stop smoking.

According to Badan Pusat Statistik (BPS), 3.69% of the teenagers in Indonesia (age below 18) smoked, both tobacco and electronic cigarettes in 2021. From 2015-2021, the trend line of the smokers is stable, except for 2018. In 2018, there is a big blast, at 9.65%. According to our analysis, this blast can happen because the Indonesian government just legalised vape. This creates a trend about vaping, thus increasing the number of teen smokers in Indonesia. When classified into several age groups, 16-18 have the highest percentage, at 9.59%. Then, followed by 13-15, at 1.44%. Last is 10-12 at 0.07%. We think that this number is a lot. According to BPS, the number of people below 18 is 88400000. If 3.69% of them smoked, then there will be 3261960 people smoking. Even though the percentage is small, it doesn’t mean that the result will be small either.

Survey Result

These charts illustrate the result of a survey in which Santa Laurensia students were asked whether they are a passive smoker or not, including sex of the passive smokers, the grade the passive smokers at, the age of the passive smokers, the frequency of them inhaling smoke, and what they do when there is someone smoking near them.

From 80 students participating in the survey, it is clear that the majority of Santa Laurensia students don’t become passive smokers, at 72.5%. The rest, at 22.5%, are passive smokers. The majority of the passive smokers are female, at 81.8%, and the rest are male. They are mostly seventh graders, at 50%. Eight grade has the second highest students, at 36.4%. The grade with the least students is senior highschoolers, at 13.6%. The majority age of the passive smokers in Santa Laurensia is 13 years, at 54.6%. The 12 years group represents 22.7% of the total. The 16-18 years group shares the third highest students, at 13.6%. The age group with the least students is 14 years, at 9.1%. The frequency of inhaling cigarette’s smoke by the passive smokers is dominated by sometimes, at 45.5%. The seldom group has the second highest percentage, at 31.8%, followed by often, at 18.2%. The smallest frequency group that has the least student is always, at 4.5%. Lastly, if the students are near a person smoking, the majority will avoid them, at 77.3%. The rest of them will ignore them and stay there.

These charts illustrate the result of a survey in which Santa Laurensia students were asked whether they are ever smoked or not, including sex of the smokers, the grade the smokers at, the age of thesmokers, the type of cigarettes they smoked, the reason why they smoked, the number of cigarettes they smoked in their entire life, and the last time they smoked.

From 80 students participating in the survey, it is clear that the majority of Santa Laurensia students never smoked, at 96.3%. The rest, at 3.7%, are ever smoked. The majority of the smokers are male, two-thirds of the total, and the rest are male, a third of the total. They are mostly senior highschoolers, two-thirds of the total. The grade with the least students is seventh grade, a third of the total. The majority age of the passive smokers in Santa Laurensia is 16-18 years, two-thirds of the total. The age group with the least student contribution is 12 years, a third of the total. All of the smokers ever tried smoking using electronic cigarettes but only two-thirds of the total ever tried smoking using tobacco cigarettes. Two-thirds of the smokers smoked because of curiosity, while the rest smoked because smoking is a fun activity to do with friends. The majority of the smokers, two thirds of the total, smoked 1-10 cigarettes in their entire life. On the other hand, the other third smoked 11-100 cigarettes in his entire life. Two thirds of the smokers last smoked over 1 year ago, while the other one third last smoked within this month.
